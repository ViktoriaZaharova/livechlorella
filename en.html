Difference of Live Chlorela from powders and tablets

In developed countries, dry Chlorella in the form of tablets and powders is obtained from soil strains discovered in the 60s of the last century.

Such strains of Chlorella are common in Nature everywhere, their cells are protected by a strong shell and for this reason, soil strains are difficult to digest by the body.

In addition, in the manufacture of powders and tablets - drying cells at temperatures above 200 degrees and storage objectively occur loss of the composition of BAS.

It is obvious that the effect of powders or tablets from dry Chlorella will be fundamentally lower than products based on living microalgae cells.

What is unique about our strain?

The strain of Chlorella IGF # C-111 isolated in 1977 from the Nurek reservoir is not soil, but planktonic.

The inflow of the reservoir is determined by the Vash river, which originates in the Alpine regions of the Pamir-Alai. For this reason, the Nurek reservoir is fed only by snow, glacial and rain water sources.

Such natural conditions had a decisive influence on the cultural properties of the native strain of Chlorella.

The presence of a thin shell of the strain determines not only the planktonic properties, but also the digestibility of the BAS composition of the microalgae cell.
